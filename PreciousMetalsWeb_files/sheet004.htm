                    Gold at the BIS - July 2025

The Bank for International Settlements' activity in the gold market is described at length in their Annual Reports as they offer tailored services to central banks with regards to active or passive management of gold reserves. In their most recent Annual Report (cob March 2025) they note that BIS' own gold remained stable at 102 tons, whilst gold deposits (from central banks to the BIS) had increased by 36 tons to 426 tons. Meanwhile 'off balance sheet' gold (gold which is safekept at the BIS for central banks as part of their custodial services) had increased by 61 tons to 445 tonnes.		          
       
         
                 
                 
                 
                 
                 
                 
        As for their regular markets activity, the BIS also discloses open position for their Gold Swap trades.
These gold swaps are dealt with their counterparties under stringent ISDA/CSA agreements and allow them to yield returns on their USD holdings by lending dollars and taking in Gold loco Bank of England. It is just another money-market tool for central banks to manage actively their currencies and optimize achievable yields. In March 2025 the BIS had an open position of just 10 tons (down 63 tons year on year).

From BIS' Statements of Accounts updated monthly at this page, we can reverse-engineer their gold swap data and get updated monthly figures for gold swaps, assuming that their 'own gold' portion remains stable at 102 tons overtime. In turn, this gives traders a small window of reference for the activity of the broader official sector in gold swaps.
          
                 
                 
                 
                 
                 
                 
                 
                 
        At close of July 31st, the BIS has 33 tons (1 million ounces) of outstanding gold swaps with bullion banks. Unsurprisingly, it's a drop of 8 tons from the previous month since gold swap rates went steady across the month between 4.00% and 4.50% 1m to 1y. The implied lease rates of between 10 and 20 bps are not enough for the BIS to increase gold swaps volumes as they mostly get better yields for their USD through other money-market products.
