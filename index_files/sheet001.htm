Gold futures wrapped the week at $3989.2, reflecting a decline of $39.5 over the past five days, impacted by a firmer dollar and evolving Fed outlook amid concerns of a potential US government shutdown. Open interest in futures rose slightly, increasing by 843 lots to 457,021, while ETF holdings dropped significantly by 344,892 ounces to 97,045,550 ounces. COMEX inventories also saw a reduction, down 578,460 ounces to 37,881,682 ounces.

EFP volumes have been relatively stable, with 670 lots traded yesterday and 4,670 lots so far this month, suggesting a consistent interest in EFP trades despite the recent volatility. The December 2025 EFP is currently at $7.81 mids, yielding 3.36%, compared to 4.17% for the OTC equivalent, presenting an attractive $1.9 arb opportunity. Gold swap rates remain orderly, running inverted from 4.15% to 3.65%, while lease rates are stable around -0.10%. Overall, the market shows resilience, albeit with cautious trading dynamics.
