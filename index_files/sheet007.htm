                  LBMA Vaults Data / Silver - Aug 2025                      
       
 
        The amount of silver held in the London vaults at the end of August rose by 14.4m ounces month on month to 792.4m ounces. Of these, around 592.1m ounces are holdings earmarked for ETFs and other private holders (figure calculated via own ventilation of all existing ETFs custodian vault location).
In effect, the 'free-float' of silver in London's vaults comes lower, at 200.3m ounces, down by 2m ounces from the previous month.
👉 ETFs and custodial holdings grew sharply last month, with demand adding 16.4m ounces and siphoning off liquidity.

In contrast, the LBMA OTC Trade Data shows that an average of 375m ounces silver changed hands every day in the week to Aug-11. This suggests a shortfall in available unallocated silver with the free-float effectively running a negative balance of 174.7m ounces and implying dealers are borrowing to cover their metal balances.

          ANALYZING THE FLOAT
 
               
                                   
                  Silver's London free-float is ~26% of vault holdings, yet it now covers <0.5 day of OTC spot trading, the lowest on record. Float rotation has surged to ~2 times per day, meaning the same ounces are being recycled multiple times just to meet flow. This is liquidity by velocity, not stock.                  
                                   
                                   
                                   
                  Since Dec-24, the float has sat below LBMA clearing volumes, leaving a coverage ratio <1 day for settled ounces. Tight borrowing conditions (lease rates jumping as SIFO compresses) leaves the market highly vulnerable to any shift toward physical allocation or fresh ETF creations.                  
                                   
                                   
                                   
                                   
                  LBMA clearing data shows the float does not average daily settlements. In a stress scenario, where investors demand physical allocation, the float would be exhausted in just half a settlement day, underscoring how fragile silver's market structure remains beneath the surface.
