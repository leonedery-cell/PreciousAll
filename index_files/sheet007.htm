                  LBMA Vaults Data / Silver - Aug 2025                  
       
 
        The amount of silver held in the London vaults at the end of August decreased by -2.1m ounces month on month to 790.3m ounces. Of these, around 611.1m ounces are holdings earmarked for ETFs and other private holders (figure calculated via own ventilation of all existing ETFs custodian vault location).
In effect, the 'free-float' of silver in London's vaults comes lower, at 179.2m ounces, down by 21.1m ounces from the previous month.
👉 ETFs and custodial holdings grew sharply last month, with demand adding 19m ounces and siphoning off liquidity.

In contrast, the LBMA OTC Trade Data shows that an average of 532.6m ounces silver changed hands every day in the week to Oct-06. This suggests a shortfall in available unallocated silver with the free-float effectively running a negative balance of 353.4m ounces and implying dealers are borrowing to cover their metal balances.

          ANALYZING THE FLOAT
 
           
                               
                  Silver's London free-float is ~23% of vault holdings, yet it now covers just one-third of a day of OTC spot trading, the lowest on record. Float rotation has surged to ~3 times per day, meaning the same ounces are being recycled multiple times just to meet flow. This is liquidity by velocity, not stock.              
                               
                               
                               
                  Since Dec-24, the float has sat below LBMA clearing volumes, leaving a coverage ratio of half a day for settled ounces. Tight borrowing conditions (lease rates jumping as SIFO compresses) leaves the market highly vulnerable to any shift toward physical allocation or fresh ETF creations.              
                               
                               
                               
                               
                  LBMA clearing data shows the float does not cover daily settlements. In a stress scenario, where investors demand physical allocation, the float would be exhausted in just half a settlement day, underscoring how fragile silver's market structure remains beneath the surface.
